Biology

Hoping to get some science to the people.

My actual day job involves figuring out how germs (ok, "bacteria") that live inside soybean plants manage to swim through the soil. Personally, I think this is incredibly fascinating and really important work! But let's be honest, the only people who care about this are other microbiologists/biophysicists.

But I do care very much about getting science to the people, about understanding the interactions between science and society, and generally just think the natural world is crazy awesome.

Published Writings

Coming very soon: The History of Xenopus as a Model Organism
Of all life on Earth, there are only about a dozen "model organisms" that dominate biological study. One of them, the African clawed frog, or Xenopus, has a rather colorful history. Asimov Press, 2026.
Coming less soon: Looking at Macomolecules
In the past few decades, scientists have developed brilliant tricks to find where specific DNA, RNA, and proteins can be found in a cell. But finding speific lipids and other biomolecules is still a challenge.
Coming hopefully someday: Rhizosphere Engineering
Without industrial production of ammonium, there would not be nearly enough food to feed everyone alive. But bacteria have a better way, and maybe we can copy them.

Interactive Models

Bret Victor asks people to stop drawing dead fish. Applied to biology, Jason Somers complains how we teach it all wrong; with lots of facts and names instead of as dynaimc technologies of mind-blowing capabilities. Thanks to the marvels of GenAI, we can do a lot better than teaching biology with dead fish, we can even do better than showing videos of swimming fish. I made a few simple examples to show what science education could look like in the era of LLMs. Until then, every science teacher should be using NetLogo and similar tools.

Chemotaxis
How bacteria navigate chemical gradients using a biased random walk. Compare random vs. chemotactic behavior in real time.
Alpha Helix Architect
Build a polypeptide chain and see if it forms a stable alpha helix. Learn how amino acid properties affect protein folding.
